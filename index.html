Divine.
Mount Fuji

For the Japanese, Mount Fuji symbolizes purity, perseverance, and eternity. Its steep slopes and snowy peaks inspire respect and contemplation. In Japanese culture, the mountain is often associated with meditation and the search for spiritual enlightenment.

Spring.
Cherry Blossom Tree

For many Japanese, the blooming of the cherry blossom trees symbolizes human life, transience, and nobleness. The Japanese love to celebrate and cherish the cherry blossom trees during the limited flowering period, and many people hold 'flower watching' parties known as ' hanami '.

Sacred.
Torii

Since ancient times, the traditional Japanese gate is most commonly found at the entrance of or within a Shinto shrine, where it symbolically marks the transition from the mundane to the sacred and a spot where kami are welcomed and thought to travel through.

Fortune.
Maneki Neko

Cats are revered in Japan as they are believed to bring good luck; this amiable figure is called "Maneki Neko" (welcoming cats). The Beckoning cat displayed at the entrance in shops or homes is a symbol of flourishing business. The pose of the cat figurine, which raises one hand and invites a person, means the exact behavior to call many customers into the said shop.
